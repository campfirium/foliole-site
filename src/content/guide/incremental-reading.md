Incremental reading is a way to keep reading material in motion instead of treating every article, book, or note as something that must be finished in one sitting.

In Foliole, this idea is expressed as a reading workspace. A source can stay in the library, return through scheduling, and gradually become smaller pieces of durable material: highlighted passages, cloze deletions, review items, or references for later work.

The important boundary is that Foliole is not trying to turn every text into flashcards. Some material should simply be revisited. Some should be extracted. Some should be discarded. The Guide uses this page as the starting concept because many other Foliole features make more sense once reading is understood as an ongoing flow.

## Why it matters

Most reading systems make users choose between two weak defaults: finish the material now, or lose track of it. Incremental reading gives the system a third option: keep the material available, but let attention return in smaller, better-timed passes.

## How Foliole uses it

Foliole combines a document library, a reading pane, extraction tools, and scheduling. The result is not a generic document viewer and not only a spaced repetition app. It is a workspace for turning reading into something that can continue.
